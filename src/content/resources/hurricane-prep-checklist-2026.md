# Hurricane Season 2026 Prep Checklist

**Atlantic hurricane season officially runs June 1 – November 30, 2026.** The mistake almost everyone makes: waiting for a named storm to appear before stocking up. By then shelves are empty and gas lines are long. The fix is one quiet afternoon, now, working down a list.

This is that list. Print it, check the boxes, done for the season.

## 1. Water & food (3+ days, ideally 7)

- [ ] **1 gallon of water per person, per day** (don't forget pets)
- [ ] Non-perishable food: canned goods, peanut butter, crackers, dried fruit
- [ ] Manual can opener
- [ ] Baby formula / special diet items
- [ ] Coolers + ice packs (freeze water bottles now)

## 2. Power & light

- [ ] Flashlights + spare batteries (not candles — fire risk)
- [ ] Power bank, fully charged; car charger
- [ ] Battery or hand-crank radio (NOAA weather band)
- [ ] If you have a generator: test it, store fuel **outside**, never run indoors

## 3. The go-bag (one per person)

- [ ] Medications (7-day supply) + a written list of doses
- [ ] Copies of ID, insurance, deed/lease in a **waterproof bag**
- [ ] Cash in small bills (ATMs and cards fail when power does)
- [ ] First-aid kit
- [ ] Change of clothes, sturdy shoes, rain poncho
- [ ] Phone, chargers, paper map of evacuation routes

## 4. Home, before the wind

- [ ] Clear gutters and drains
- [ ] Bring in or tie down anything that can fly (furniture, grills, plants)
- [ ] Trim dead branches near the house
- [ ] Know where your water/gas/electric shutoffs are
- [ ] Fill the bathtub for flushing water if a storm is imminent
- [ ] Photograph each room for insurance **before** any damage

## 5. Documents & info

- [ ] Insurance policy numbers + claims phone line
- [ ] Photos of valuables and serial numbers
- [ ] Emergency contacts written on paper (don't rely on your phone)
- [ ] Out-of-state contact everyone checks in with
- [ ] Local evacuation zone — look yours up today, not during a warning

## 6. Don't forget

- [ ] Pets: carrier, food, vaccination records, a current photo
- [ ] Babies & elderly: extra supplies, comfort items
- [ ] Car: full tank by the time a storm is named
- [ ] Whistle (to signal for help if trapped)

## Know the difference: Watch vs Warning

- **Hurricane Watch** — conditions *possible* within 48 hours. Start prepping.
- **Hurricane Warning** — conditions *expected* within 36 hours. Finish prepping or evacuate.
